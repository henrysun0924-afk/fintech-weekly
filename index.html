Internal Research · US Credit InfrastructureUpdated Apr 2026USMortgage Credit / BureausFICO / EXPN / EFX / TRU
FICO doubles mortgage wholesale to $10 in 2026, but bureaus — led by Experian — capture 60-65% of tri-merge economics; Lender Choice is form, not substance
  • Mortgage pricing — ~10x in 7 years: FICO wholesale mortgage price has gone from ~$0.50 (1989-2018) to $4.95 (Nov-2024) to $10/file in 2026 via the Mortgage Direct License Program (alt: $4.95 + $32 success fee — same UE). Reseller all-in cost tracking from ~$60 toward $80+; triggered Sen. Hawley antitrust push and repeated public criticism from FHFA Director Pulte.
  • Bureaus take the bigger slice: Of a $80-100 tri-merge file, FICO captures ~$14.85 (~15-18%); the three bureaus take 60-65% of the chain. In consumer credit (cards / personal loans), FICO has not raised price in 5 years while bureaus have quietly re-priced via finer data cadence (refresh 45d→30d, more attributes) at single-digit CAGR.
  • Lender Choice doesn't break the moat: FHFA (Pulte) kept tri-merge and adds VantageScore 4.0 as a permitted alternative, targeted live summer 2026. But 98.8% of outstanding MBS reference FICO; VantageScore MBS-channel penetration <5%. Synchrony's 2021 switch took 3 years and ~$200mn and only applies to subprime — switching costs are the binding constraint.
  • EXPN is the standout bureau: EBIT margin 28% with 5-yr profit CAGR 12% (vs EFX 25% / 6% and TRU 24% / 7%). Most diversified revenue (financials only 39% — the lowest), strongest 2C (28% of revenue, 200mn+ users, 12mn paying members at $15-25/month in NA), and cloud migration largely done by FY26 (>85% on cloud), removing the historic 800-900bp annual drag and unlocking further margin upside.
  • LatAm read-across: Serasa (Experian Brazil, 65% share) sustains EBIT margin >30% and is opening up feature-data commercialization (Quod has already moved — 50 attributes priced at R$0.5, same as one model score); 2026 push to onboard Serasa is the next leg. Mexico stays a closed duopoly (Buró 60% / Círculo 40%, Buró EBITDA margin 48%) with no pricing relief in 5 years.
